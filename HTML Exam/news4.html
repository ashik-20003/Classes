India buys more american oil.

India has stepped up purchases of American crude in recent weeks as refiners seek to diversify their supply sources and capitalize on competitive pricing. Officials say the move reflects strengthening energy ties between the two countries and an effort to balance imports amid global market volatility. Analysts note the additional U.S. cargoes will help cushion domestic fuel supplies, though long-term trends will depend on freight costs, refining margins, and evolving geopolitical dynamics.
